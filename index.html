Oracle Bones to LLMs

Ask one question. Receive two answers. One emerges through ritualized divination. The other arrives as model-like synthesis. Then decide which feels wiser, more trustworthy, more intelligent, and more useful.

Framing
This compares two cultural technologies for producing interpretable answers under uncertainty.
Not the claim
Oracle bones were not “early AI.” The comparison is about authority, interpretation, and what kinds of outputs a culture learns to trust.
Question behind the project
Does “intelligence” belong to the machine, or to the style of answer we are trained to recognize as credible?

Historical note: Shang oracle bones were used in ritual divination and preserved early Chinese inscriptions. Turing’s imitation game defines intelligence behaviorally through text exchange; recent scholarship argues large AI models are cultural and social technologies.

Built as a course artifact for thinking about whether machine intelligence is a neutral property, or a culturally shaped judgment about what kinds of answers count as meaningful, credible, or wise.
